WHY FUCKTHEINTERNET?
BCAUSE FUCK THE INTERNET!
My teenage years marked the end of what I consider the "golden age" of socials media. These websites and apps I once visited to see what my friends were sharing, or to find new people to follow and talk to and be inspired by, were changed dramatically in the algorithm age. it seems like more and more, social media is becoming harder to divorce from the internet as a whole. Images and articles hosted by social media sites dominate search algorithms.
Many of us were told that the internet is forever, but it's been proven many times this isn't true. The internet, as a library, is a collaborative effort. If something is reposted, hosted by multiple servers, and maintained, it's safe to say it will be around for a while. But things do get forgotten, servers shut down, websites get wiped, posts go unarchived. The things we lose often seem to be whatever doesn't pay into social media's engagement economy - stuff that doesn't get reposted, rehosted, or reshared. What do we stand to lose when what is deemed less popular is forgotten?
